Elektron Synthesis - Part 4: Machinedrum TRX-CY Cymbal and TRX-CB Cowbell

The TRX-CY and TRX-CB are the Machinedrum's analog-modeled cymbal and cowbell. The TRX-CY uses a dense inharmonic oscillator bank with ring-modulated partials and a very long multi-stage decay envelope. The TRX-CB is a direct descendant of the Roland TR-808 cowbell — two square wave oscillators through a resonant bandpass filter and a gated amplitude envelope. Both are illuminating targets for Pd recreation because they expose how a small number of techniques produce very recognisable sounds.

TRX-CY Synthesis Architecture

A real cymbal produces a very dense, irregular partial spectrum from the complex vibration modes of a metal disc. The synthesis model approximates this with:

  • Six or more oscillators at inharmonic frequencies
  • Ring modulation between pairs of oscillators (produces sum/difference frequencies, further densifying the spectrum)
  • Highpass filter to remove sub-bass content
  • Dual-band decay (low frequencies decay faster than high — opposite of hi-hat)
; Oscillator bank frequencies (base values, Hz):
; These produce the MD-style cymbal spectrum:
203, 308, 411, 607, 893, 1214, 1823, 2431

; With FREQ offset: each + frq

; Plus ring-modulated pairs:
; RM1: osc[0] * osc[2] → produces sums/diffs at 203±411 Hz = 208, 614
; RM2: osc[1] * osc[3] → produces sums/diffs at 308±607 Hz = 299, 915
; RM3: osc[4] * osc[5] → produces sums/diffs at 893±1214 Hz = 321, 2107
; controlled by METAL parameter

; Ring modulation in Pd:
[osc~ 203+frq]   → $oscA
[osc~ 411+frq]   → $oscC

$oscA
|
[*~]   ← $oscC           ; multiply = ring modulator (both -1 to +1)
|
[*~ $metal]              ; blend: 0 = no ring mod, 1 = full ring mod

; Mix ring-modulated pairs with direct oscillators:
[+~] direct_osc_sum
[+~] ring_mod_sum × metal
→ oscillator_bus

Dual-Band Decay

On a real cymbal, high-frequency content decays faster than low frequency — the opposite of a hi-hat, where the bright sizzle sustains. Split the oscillator bus into a low band and a high band with independent envelopes: 

oscillator_bus
    │
    ├── LOW band: [lop~ 2000] → [vline~] dcy envelope → [*~ tn]
    │
    └── HIGH band: [hip~ 2000] → [vline~] hdcy envelope → [*~ 1-tn]

With TONE at 0.5, both bands get equal weight. Moving toward 1.0 emphasises the low band (darker, bell-like cymbal); moving toward 0.0 emphasises high frequencies (brighter, sizzlier cymbal).

Plus the noise component, highpass filtered and decaying at a rate between dcy and hdcy: 

[noise~]
→ [hip~ 3000]              ; remove low rumble
→ [vline~] decay at (dcy + hdcy) / 2
→ [*~ nse]

Frequency Sweep

The SWEEP parameter adds a brief upward pitch excursion at the moment of attack — the physical analogy is the initial high-frequency impact of a stick on the cymbal edge before the resonant body takes over: 

; On trigger: all oscillator frequencies momentarily offset upward
; then return to base over 10–30 ms

; Apply to all oscillators via a shared pitch offset signal:
[vline~]    ; freq offset: swp*100 0, 0 30 0    (30 ms sweep down)
|
[sig~ 0]   ; bias
|
[+~]       ; = offset bus

; Each oscillator receives: baseFreq + freqOffset
; In Pd, message-rate frequencies don't support signal addition directly;
; use [expr~] or [sig~] to add the sweep offset to each oscillator's freq:
[osc~]  ← [+~ baseFreq offset]   ; where baseFreq is a [sig~] from stored float

TRX-CB Synthesis Architecture

The TR-808 cowbell is the classic reference for synthesised cowbell. It uses two square wave oscillators — one at the fundamental and one at a fixed interval — mixed and fed through a resonant bandpass filter. The resonant filter is what gives the cowbell its metallic, pinging quality. The TRX-CB follows this architecture: 

; Two square wave oscillators:
[phasor~ $tune]
|
[*~ 2][-~ 1]          ; phasor → sawtooth → sign comparison → square
|
(clip~ -1 1 after *200 overdrive)
|
[*~ 0.5]   → oscA

[phasor~ $tune * $beat]   ; Osc B at beat × tune (default ≈1.47 for 808-style)
|
(same square conversion)
|
[*~ 0.5]   → oscB

; Tone crossfade: 0 = full square, 1 = sine blend
; At tn=0: hard square wave (buzzy)
; At tn=1: blend with a sine at same frequency (softer, bell-like)
[osc~ $tune]     ; sine version of OscA
|
[*~ tn]
→ sineBlend

[+~ oscA * (1-tn), sineBlend]   → oscAMixed
[+~ oscB * (1-tn), oscBSine]    → oscBMixed

oscAMixed + oscBMixed → oscillator_sum

The two oscillators are summed (not ring-modulated — that would produce sums/differences, whereas cowbell sounds are the direct mix of the two pitches): 

oscillator_sum
|
[bp~ $flt $res]         ; resonant bandpass — the key cowbell shaper
|
[tanh waveshaper dst]
|
[*~]  ← [vline~] amplitude: 1 2, 0 $dcy 2    ; decay envelope
|
[*~ level * vel]
|
[outlet~]

The Bandpass Filter as Identity

The resonant bandpass filter is what makes a cowbell recognisable. Without it (flat frequency response), the two oscillators just sound like a harsh buzzing tone. With the bandpass applied, the filter's resonance selects a narrow frequency band and rings it, producing the characteristic "ting" quality.

The filter cutoff (FLT) and resonance (RES) parameters define the cowbell's identity:

  • Low cutoff (500–800 Hz), high resonance (0.8–1.0): deep, metallic cowbell
  • Mid cutoff (1000–2000 Hz), medium resonance (0.5–0.7): classic 808 cowbell
  • High cutoff (3000–6000 Hz), medium resonance (0.5): bright, bell-like

In Pd, the [bp~] object takes two arguments: center frequency and Q. The MD's resonance parameter maps approximately as: Q = res * 15 + 1 (range 1–16).

BEAT Parameter

The BEAT parameter controls the frequency ratio between the two oscillators. The TR-808 cowbell uses a ratio of approximately 1:1.47 (the second oscillator is 1.47× the fundamental). This produces an inharmonic beating that gives the cowbell its unstable, metallic shimmer. The TRX-CB exposes this ratio as a parameter: 

; BEAT range 0.8–2.0:
; 0.8: near-unison (close beating, almost chorus)
; 1.47: classic 808-style cowbell
; 2.0: octave (more bell-like, less cowbell)

Summary

The TRX-CY uses a dense inharmonic oscillator bank with ring modulation between pairs (controlled by METAL), dual-band independent decay envelopes (low/high via TONE), and a frequency sweep on attack. The TRX-CB follows the TR-808 cowbell architecture: two square-wave oscillators at an inharmonic interval, summed and run through a resonant bandpass filter. The bandpass filter is the key identity element — it rings the selected frequency band, producing the characteristic metallic cowbell ping.
