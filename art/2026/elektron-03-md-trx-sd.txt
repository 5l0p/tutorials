Elektron Synthesis - Part 3: Machinedrum TRX-SD Snare and TRX-XT Hi-Hats
2026-01-01


The TRX-SD and TRX-HH/XT are the Machinedrum's analog-modeled snare and hi-hat
machines. The TRX-SD uses a dual-resonator body fed through a noise shaper, with
independently decaying body and snap components. The TRX-HH and TRX-XT share the
same oscillator bank — only their default decay times differ, and triggering a TRX-HH
while a TRX-XT is open chokes the open hat tail. This article recreates both in Pd.

TRX-SD Synthesis Architecture


; TRX-SD signal flow:

TRIGGER
  │
  ├─ [BODY OSC] ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  │   Two detuned sine oscillators:
  │     Osc A: freq = tone
  │     Osc B: freq = tone * 1.52  (resonance mode above fundamental)
  │   Both swept from (tone + sweep) to tone over sdcy ms
  │   Amplitude: [vline~] 0→1→0 over dcy ms
  │   [+~][*~ 0.5] → body bus
  │
  ├─ [SNAP NOISE] ────────────────────────────────────────────
  │   [noise~]
  │   → [bp~ tone*8 2.0]            ; bandpass at 8× tone freq
  │   → [hip~ tone*3]               ; highpass removes body content
  │   Amplitude: [vline~] 0→1→0 over hdcy ms   ; fast snap
  │   [*~ sn]
  │
  ├─ [BODY NOISE] ────────────────────────────────────────────
  │   [noise~]
  │   → [lop~ tone*2]               ; lowpass at 2× tone freq
  │   Amplitude: [vline~] 0→1→0 over dcy*0.6 ms ; slower than snap
  │   [*~ sn * 0.4]
  │
  [+~] sum
  │
  [tanh waveshaper dst]
  │
  [*~ level * vel]
  │
  [outlet~]


The Dual-Resonator Body


The TRX-SD body uses two sine oscillators at a non-integer frequency ratio — not
an octave (2:1) or fifth (3:2), but approximately 1.52:1. This slight inharmonicity
gives the body a slightly "wooden" or complex pitched quality compared to a single
sine. A real snare head resonates at multiple inharmonic modes; the 1.52 ratio
approximates the relationship between the (0,1) and (1,1) membrane modes:



[r $0-tone]        ; base frequency Hz
|
[t f f]
|        |
[f]    [* 1.52]    ; Osc B is 1.52× Osc A
|        |
[osc~] [osc~]
|        |
[*~]   [*~]        ; each × its own amplitude vline~
|        |
[+~]               ; sum
|
[*~ 0.5]           ; normalise



Both oscillators receive the same pitch sweep (same vline~ ramp sent to both),
keeping them in ratio throughout the sweep.


The Two Noise Paths


The TRX-SD has two noise paths with independent decay times and filter positions:



Snap noise (HDECAY): bandpass filtered at high frequencies (8× tone), with a
highpass removing everything below ~3× tone. This is the tight, snappy "crack" of the
stick hitting the head. It decays fast (5–500 ms controlled by HDECAY).



Body noise (DECAY × 0.6): lowpass filtered at 2× tone, contributing a warm
mid-frequency noise underpinning the body oscillators. It decays more slowly and at
lower amplitude than the snap noise.



The SNAPPY (sn) parameter scales the combined noise level. Setting sn=0 gives a
pure tonal body; sn=1 with short hdcy gives a pure noise crack; balanced values produce
the classic snare character:



; Snap noise path:
[noise~]
|
[bp~ $toneX8 2.0]
|
[hip~ $toneX3]
|
[*~]    ← [vline~]: 1 2, 0 $hdcy 2   ; snap envelope
|
[*~ $sn]

; Body noise path:
[noise~]
|
[lop~ $toneX2]
|
[*~]    ← [vline~]: 1 2, 0 $dcy*0.6 2
|
[*~ $sn * 0.4]      ; body noise at 40% of snap level


TRX-HH and TRX-XT


The TRX-HH (closed hi-hat) and TRX-XT (open hi-hat, "XT" for eXTended) use identical
oscillator bank and filter architectures — only their default decay times differ.
They can be placed on separate tracks and the TRX-HH can choke the TRX-XT.

TRX-HH Synthesis Architecture


The TRX-HH uses six square-wave oscillators at inharmonic frequencies — the same
metallic oscillator bank model as the Series 9 hi-hat but with a slightly different
set of base frequencies tuned to Elektron's house sound:



; MD-style hi-hat oscillator frequencies (base offsets, Hz):
; These approximate the MD's characteristic metallic spectrum:
291, 398, 453, 587, 619, 800

; With FREQ offset added (0–600 Hz shifts entire bank up):
291+frq, 398+frq, 453+frq, 587+frq, 619+frq, 800+frq



Each oscillator is a hard-clipped sine (approximating a square wave):



[osc~ 291+frq]
|
[*~ 200]             ; overdrive → square
|
[clip~ -1 1]

; × 6 oscillators, all summed and normalised



The TONE parameter crossfades between a bright (all oscillators equal) and dark (lower
oscillators boosted, upper attenuated) mix:



; TONE crossfade: low tone = lower freqs dominant, high tone = upper freqs
; Simple approximation: apply a per-oscillator gain curve:
gain_i = 1 - (i/6) * (1 - tn)   ; 0 ≤ i 


The oscillator sum then goes through a highpass filter (HPFREQ) and a noise blend:



oscillator sum
|
[hip~ $hpf]          ; removes all content below hpf Hz
|
[+~] ← [noise~] × [hip~ hpf] × nse    ; noise at same HP point
|
[tanh waveshaper dst]
|
[*~] ← [vline~] dcy amplitude envelope
|
[*~ level * vel]
|
[outlet~]


Choke Implementation


The choke mechanism on the MD allows one machine to cut off (choke) another. When
a TRX-HH trigger fires, it sends a "choke" message to any TRX-XT on the same choke
group, causing the open hat's amplitude envelope to immediately ramp to zero:



; TRX-HH, on trigger:
; 1. Fire own envelopes normally
; 2. Send choke to group:
[bang]
|
[t b b]
|        |
fire own   [s $0-choke-out]    ; broadcast to choke bus

; TRX-XT, receiver:
[r $0-choke-in]    ; set to same choke group as HH
|
[bang]
|
[0 5(              ; ramp amplitude to 0 over 5 ms (fast choke)
→ amplitude [vline~]



In the Lua/host sequencer, the choke group is managed by the pattern: when step N has
a HH trigger, also send a choke to any XT that is "open" (still in its decay).


TRX-XT vs TRX-HH


The only difference in the Pd patches is the default decay time range: TRX-HH defaults
to 5–200 ms (closed), TRX-XT defaults to 200–10000 ms (open). The receiver namespace
uses separate prefixes ($0-hh- vs $0-xt-), allowing them to
be instantiated simultaneously on different tracks.


Summary


The TRX-SD combines two inharmonically-tuned body oscillators (1:1.52 ratio) with two
noise paths — a fast snappy highpass-filtered snap, and a slower lowpass body noise.
The TRX-HH/XT uses a six-oscillator inharmonic metallic bank with a highpass and noise
blend. The TRX-HH chokes the TRX-XT by ramping its amplitude envelope to zero on
trigger. All parameters are p-lock ready via $0- receivers.
